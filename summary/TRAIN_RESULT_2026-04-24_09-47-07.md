## Diagnosis

The H5 labels generated after the 3-class fusion already contain many `ice` pixels as class `2`:

- train raw H5 CLP distribution: clear `13.90%`, water `38.93%`, ice `47.17%`
- val raw H5 CLP distribution: clear `17.63%`, water `35.40%`, ice `46.97%`
- test raw H5 CLP distribution: clear `17.81%`, water `34.29%`, ice `47.90%`

But the Dataset compatibility remap only kept `{0, 1, 4}` and therefore converted current `2=ice` labels to `NaN`. Training then saw only clear/water:

- logged training distribution after Dataset remap: `c0=26.3% | c1=73.7% | c2=0.0%`

This explains why the model predicted almost all `water` and never learned `ice`.

## Planned fix

- Preserve current 3-class `2=ice` labels in `CLP_LABEL_REMAP`.
- Keep old 5-class compatibility by also mapping old `4=ice` to new `2=ice`.
- Add split-date filtering to Dataset so `TRAIN_DATES / VAL_DATES / TEST_DATES` control both fusion and training/evaluation.
- Run a small experiment with 3 train days, 1 val day, and 1 test day.
